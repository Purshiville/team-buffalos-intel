Glossary — Key Terms Explained

A reference guide to the industry terms, product names and abbreviations used throughout this training sheet.

Products & Plans

AIO — All-In-One Plan
Sanlam Sky's flagship combined product. Includes funeral cover (Plans 1–9, R10 000–R90 000), up to R500 000 non-underwritten life cover for the main member and spouse, double accident cover on all lives, Cash Back after 15 years, no-more-premiums benefit, and burial repatriation. No medicals or blood tests required.
ILC — Immediate Life Cover
Sanlam Sky's standalone life cover product. Pays up to R2 000 000 on death or permanent physical impairment. No waiting period, no medicals, no blood tests, no occupational exclusions. Cover starts the day after approval. Available for ages 18–60. Underwritten by Sanlam Developing Markets (FSP 11230).
Value Funeral Plan
Sanlam Sky's entry-level funeral plan. Suited for older lives or clients needing basic cover at lower premiums.
Enhanced Priority Funeral Plan
Sanlam Sky's funeral plan designed for higher-risk extended family cover situations.
Essential Med
Sanlam Sky's medical insurance product. NOT a medical aid — provides private medical cover access for clients who do not have medical aid. Subject to its own product rules and waiting periods.
Greenlight
Old Mutual's comprehensive adviser-sold risk platform covering life, disability, severe illness and income protection. One of the most commonly encountered competitor products in the field.
OM Protect
Old Mutual's modular risk platform for the mass-affluent market. Similar to Greenlight but with more build-your-own flexibility. Adviser-sold only.
Vitality
Discovery's health and wellness rewards programme. Clients earn status (Bronze, Silver, Gold, Diamond) through healthy behaviour, which drives premium discounts of up to 48% on Discovery Life.
POPCRU 25
Workerslife's funeral benefit policy designed specifically for POPCRU union members (South African Police Service, Correctional Services, and Courts personnel).
Assupol Prosperity / MHA
A NEHAWU union group funeral product administered by MHA Management Holdings and underwritten by Assupol Life. Appears on PERSAL payslips as 'Assupol Prosperity' or 'MHA' — often confused with a standard individual Assupol policy.
The Unlimited
A life cover and accidental injury cash benefit product sold primarily to government employees via PERSAL. NOT a funeral plan. Notably excludes pre-existing conditions permanently — including HIV, diabetes, hypertension, epilepsy and cancer.

Key Benefit Terms

Cash Back / CashBack
A benefit that returns a percentage or all of premiums paid to the policyholder after a specified period. Sky's AIO returns 100% of ALL premiums after 15 years regardless of claims. Competitor cashback products typically return smaller percentages, require no claims, or reset on any claim.
Double Accident Cover
A benefit that pays double the insured amount if the cause of death is accidental (unnatural). On Sky's AIO, this applies to ALL lives assured on ALL plans with no waiting period. Some competitors restrict this to the main member and spouse, or only on higher-tier plans.
No More Premiums Benefit
A Sky AIO benefit that waives all future premiums if the main member dies or becomes permanently disabled. All remaining members on the policy continue to be covered at no cost until the main member would have turned 65.
Waiting Period
The time after a policy starts during which a natural death claim will not be paid. The standard industry waiting period is 6 months. Accidental death is typically covered immediately. Sky's AIO waives the waiting period entirely for clients who have already served it with another licensed insurer for 31 or more consecutive days.
Waiting Period Waiver / Switch Benefit
A feature offered by several insurers (including Sky, FNB, Absa, Capitec and Liberty) that removes the new waiting period when a client switches from another licensed insurer where the waiting period has already been served.
Premium Waiver
A benefit that waives premium payments for a period after the main member dies. Duration varies by product — from 6 months (MHA, King Price) to 24 months (1Life) to 10 years / 120 months (Workerslife). Sky's AIO no-more-premiums benefit covers ALL members until the main member would have turned 65, which is often longer.
Paid-Up Benefit
Allows a policyholder to stop paying premiums at age 65 while keeping cover in force for life. Offered by Liberty and Hollard as optional add-ons. Sky's AIO no-more-premiums benefit achieves a similar outcome but triggers earlier — on death or disability of the main member rather than at a fixed age.
Repatriation
The transport of a deceased person's remains to their place of burial. Sky's AIO covers repatriation from SA, Namibia, Zimbabwe, Botswana, Swaziland, Lesotho and Mozambique. Some competitors limit repatriation to within SA borders only.
Terminal Illness Benefit
Pays out a percentage (often 95–100%) of the death benefit in advance if the life assured is diagnosed with a terminal illness and has a life expectancy of 12 months or less. Available on many life cover products including ILC, Assupol Progress, Liberty Lifestyle Protector and King Price Life.
Survivorship Benefit
Covers remaining insured family members for a period (usually 6 months) at no cost after the main policyholder dies. Offered by Absa (5-year premium requirement) and Capitec. Sky's AIO no-more-premiums benefit covers all members until age 65 — significantly longer.
Inkomo Cash Benefit
A Liberty Funeral Plus optional benefit that pays R10 000 cash for traditional cattle and catering expenses at a funeral. Culturally significant for many clients.

Distribution & Sales Terms

Adviser-Sold
A product distributed exclusively through licensed financial advisers who conduct a needs analysis before sale. Examples include Greenlight, OM Protect, Discovery Life, Liberty Lifestyle Protector and Hollard Specialist Life. Adviser-sold products generally offer more comprehensive cover but require a relationship with an adviser.
Direct / Call Centre Sale
A product sold without an adviser — typically via telephone, TV advertising, WhatsApp or online. Examples include 1Life, Clientele, Absa, FNB and Capitec. No ongoing adviser relationship or policy review after the sale.
Tied Adviser
An adviser who sells only one insurer's products. Sky advisors and Emerald Life advisers are tied advisers. Distinct from independent brokers who can sell products from multiple insurers.
Broker-Only Product
A product available exclusively through licensed independent brokers — not available direct to the public or through tied advisers. Hollard Specialist Life is a broker-only product.
FNA — Financial Needs Analysis
A structured assessment of a client's financial situation, goals, dependants, existing cover and gaps. Required before advising on or replacing any risk product. Completing a proper FNA is a compliance requirement and your most powerful sales tool.
Replacement
Cancelling or lapsing an existing policy and replacing it with a new one. Replacement conversations require disclosure of the group relationship (e.g. Assupol is now Sanlam-owned), a proper FNA, and a clear product benefit comparison. Replacing a policy must always be in the client's best interest.
Binder Holder
A company that is authorised to enter into, vary or cancel insurance policies on behalf of an insurer. The Unlimited Group is a binder holder — Centriq Life is the actual underwriter.
Underwriter
The licensed insurer that accepts the risk and is legally responsible for paying claims. The underwriter may differ from the brand the client sees — e.g. Hollard underwrites many third-party branded funeral plans without clients knowing.

Payment & Collection Terms

PERSAL
Personnel and Salary System — the South African government's payroll system used to pay public sector employees. PERSAL deductions for insurance premiums are deducted directly from salary before the employee receives their pay, making them highly reliable with very low lapse risk.
Stop Order
A deduction instruction from a government employee's salary via PERSAL. Stop orders are the primary collection method for Sanlam Sky and most competitors in the government employee market.
Debit Order
An instruction to deduct a premium directly from a bank account on a set date each month. Used by bank-tied products (FNB, Absa, Capitec) and some direct products. Higher lapse risk than PERSAL stop orders.
ACB — Automated Clearing Bureau
The South African system that processes electronic debit and credit transactions between banks, including insurance premium debit orders.
SASSA
South African Social Security Agency — the government body that administers social grants (old age, disability, child support etc.). A significant market segment for microinsurance funeral products. Clientele's cluster (including 1Life and Emerald) collectively account for over 50% of all SASSA funeral deductions.
eBucks
FNB's rewards programme. Qualifying FNB Life and funeral products earn eBucks points. Non-FNB banking clients do not benefit from this reward.

Compliance & Regulatory Terms

FSP — Financial Services Provider
A company or individual licensed by the FSCA (Financial Sector Conduct Authority) to provide financial advice and intermediary services. All insurers and advisers must hold an FSP licence. FSP numbers are referenced throughout this sheet for key competitors.
FSCA — Financial Sector Conduct Authority
South Africa's regulator for non-banking financial services. The FSCA licenses FSPs, monitors market conduct, and enforces compliance with legislation including the FAIS Act and POPIA.
FAIS Act
Financial Advisory and Intermediary Services Act. Governs how financial advice may be given in South Africa. Requires advisers to conduct a needs analysis, disclose conflicts of interest, and act in the client's best interest.
TCF — Treating Customers Fairly
A regulatory outcome-based framework requiring all financial service providers to demonstrate that fair treatment of customers is central to their business. Six outcomes guide what fair treatment looks like — from clear communication to products that perform as expected.
POPIA — Protection of Personal Information Act
South Africa's data privacy legislation. Requires that all personal information collected from clients is handled lawfully, stored securely, used only for its intended purpose, and not shared without consent.
Microinsurer
A licensed insurer that specialises in low-premium, low-benefit insurance products designed for lower-income market segments. Emerald Life is South Africa's first licensed life microinsurer.
Mutual Society
An insurer owned by its policyholders rather than shareholders. Profits are returned to policyholders rather than distributed as dividends. AVBOB is a mutual society.
Pre-Existing Condition
A medical condition that existed before a policy was taken out. Some products (notably The Unlimited) permanently exclude death claims resulting from pre-existing conditions including HIV, diabetes, hypertension, epilepsy and cancer. Sky's AIO and ILC do not require health questionnaires, so this exclusion does not apply in the same way.
